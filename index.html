KiwiCamping FAQ

What is KiwiCamping?

KiwiCamping is an offline NZ camping app with 3,500+ DOC campsites, huts, dump stations, and holiday parks. It is built for campers, vanlifers, trampers, and travellers exploring Aotearoa.

Does KiwiCamping work offline?

Yes. Download the data once and you can search, filter, and view details without coverage. Perfect for alpine passes, remote valleys, and long road trips.

How often are sites updated?

We refresh DOC, council, and NZTA data regularly and publish community updates fast so you always have current information on closures and new facilities.

Does KiwiCamping include DOC huts?

Yes. Every DOC hut includes passes, capacity, access notes, track grades, and Great Walk booking links so you can plan ahead.

Can I see freedom camping rules?

Each freedom camping zone lists local bylaws, vehicle requirements, stay limits, and enforcement tips to keep you compliant.

Does KiwiCamping list dump stations?

Dump stations and potable water points are mapped nationwide with notes on vehicle access, opening hours, and fees.

What comes with the Premium Pass?

Premium unlocks photos, live weather, MetService alerts, directions, offline trip sharing, and advanced filters. Choose a one-week or one-year pass with no subscription renewals.

Can I plan road trips?

Yes. Add sites to your trip planner, reorder stops, and share the itinerary with your travel mates. Everything stays accessible offline.

Does KiwiCamping cover hiking and tramping?

KiwiCamping lists huts, trailheads, access roads, and weather insight for Great Walks and lesser-known tramps so you can plan multi-day hikes easily.

Is there Android support?

KiwiCamping launches on iOS first. Tap the in-app waitlist to be notified when Android is ready.
